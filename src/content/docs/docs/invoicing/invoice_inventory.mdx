**Invoice Inventory** is your local library of invoices you've created. Each invoice is a gzip+base58 encoded payload saved in your browser; this page decodes and lists them, lets you track payment/delivery status, and jump to payment. Nothing here touches the chain.

**Open in the app:** [Invoice Inventory](/invoice_inventory.html).

## What it does

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Your invoices" icon="receipt">
    List every invoice you've generated.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Status tracking" icon="chart">
    Mark payment / delivery status locally.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Send for payment" icon="arrow-right">
    Link an invoice to the payment page.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## How to use

<Steps>

1. Open **Invoice Inventory** from the homepage under *Invoicing*.

2. Browse the invoices you've created (decoded from local storage).

3. Update payment/delivery status, or click **Pay** to send the invoice to [Pay Invoice](/docs/invoicing/pay_invoice.html).

</Steps>

<Aside type="note" title="Off-chain by design">
  Invoices are not on-chain objects — they're encoded strings you share out-of-band (email, chat). Only the resulting payment broadcasts a `transfer` (see [Pay Invoice](/docs/invoicing/pay_invoice.html)).
</Aside>

## Invoice data shape

The encoded invoice payload looks like:

```json
{
  "recipientId": "1.2.x",
  "recipientName": "alice",
  "identifier": "INV-2024-001",
  "note": "Thanks for your order",
  "timestamp": 1721300000000,
  "items": [
    { "id": "widget-1", "name": "Widget", "quantity": 2, "prices": [{ "asset": "USD", "price": 5 }] }
  ]
}
```
