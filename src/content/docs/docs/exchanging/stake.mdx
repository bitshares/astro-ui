**Stake** lets you provide liquidity to a BitShares liquidity pool. By depositing both sides of the pool you earn a share asset (`1.3.x`) representing your portion; withdrawing burns that share asset to reclaim the underlying assets (plus any accrued fees).

**Open in the app:** [Stake](/stake.html).

## What it does

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Deposit" icon="plus">
    Provide `amount_a` and `amount_b` to mint pool share tokens.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Withdraw" icon="minus">
    Burn share tokens to reclaim the two underlying assets.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Earn fees" icon="star">
    Your share entitles you to a cut of pool trading fees.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## How to use

<Steps>

1. Open **Stake** from the homepage under *Exchanging Funds*.

2. Select a liquidity pool (`1.19.x`). The app shows its reserves, share asset and your current stake.

3. Switch to the **Deposit** or **Withdraw** tab.

4. Enter the amounts (deposit: both assets; withdraw: share amount), review the preview, then authorize in your Beet wallet.

</Steps>

<Aside type="caution" title="Deposit ratio">
  A deposit must respect the pool's current ratio of `amount_a` to `amount_b`. If you enter an unbalanced pair the app adjusts the smaller side to match, or rejects the tx. Always review the computed counterpart amount before signing.
</Aside>

## Operations used

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="liquidity_pool_deposit">

Depositing emits `liquidity_pool_deposit` (operation `61`):

```json
{
  "account": "1.2.x",
  "pool": "1.19.x",
  "amount_a": { "amount": 10000000, "asset_id": "1.3.0" },
  "amount_b": { "amount": 5000000, "asset_id": "1.3.x" },
  "extensions": []
}
```

| Field | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `account` | Your account object id (`1.2.x`). |
| `pool` | The liquidity pool object id (`1.19.x`). |
| `amount_a` / `amount_b` | The two assets you provide, in pool order. |

  </TabItem>
  <TabItem label="liquidity_pool_withdraw">

Withdrawing emits `liquidity_pool_withdraw` (operation `62`):

```json
{
  "account": "1.2.x",
  "pool": "1.19.x",
  "share_amount": { "amount": 1234567, "asset_id": "1.3.x" },
  "extensions": []
}
```

`share_amount.asset_id` is the pool's share asset; its `precision` comes from `share_asset_details`.

  </TabItem>
</Tabs>
